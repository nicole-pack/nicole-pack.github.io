Failure and Lessons learned

The second week of coding every day went much the same as the first. There were times i felt I could code for hours and other days when it was difficult to get my thoughts straight. When I ended up taking the weekend off again, I figured it probably wasn't a bad habit to be getting into. This was a result I was expecting, although not quite so soon. I was expecting to hit a wall and become burnt out at some point, although I wasn't prepared for it. My goal was to work through the drudgery I felt; to feel burnt out, and then move on.
Despite this being an already difficult display of willpower, I ran into another problem. I found myself unable to power my will. There was something missing in all of this. At the root of my attempt to code everyday for a month, creating a website for myself, getting back into coding in general, was the need for me to get a job. Not just a want, but a need for my family to thrive. The thing that got me most excited about continuing my journey as a programmer was imagining the life I could have once my career was grown and healthy. However, these imaginations are far off, and I knew this. And when I looked around me, things looked a little more grim than they had before I started my jaunt back into coding. Why was I doing this to myself? Why was I doing this to my family?
So, I decided I would take a week off. I needed to refocus on household needs and hoped that, in the process, I would find what motivates me to be a programmer, if that motivation actually exsists. That week has come and gone, and I haven't yet started up my coding every day. But what I have come to realize is that, bottom line, what I am working toward is a future where I can provide for my family a suitable living space, enough food to eat, and the ability to be happy. These wants are simple, but I don't yet know what it takes to get there.
So, a different approach is needed. I have heard of some self taught programmers who take an approach that is a little broader than coding every day. Instead they set an hourly goal for a month. This allows days off when needed that can be balanced with days when I can get a lot done. I am excited about the prospects of actually finishing a project, and I hope I am able to get back into it soon.
